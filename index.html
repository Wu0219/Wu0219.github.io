About

I'm a researcher-engineer from Beijing, now based in Barcelona. My path started in computer vision — deep-learning semantic segmentation of satellite imagery at the Chinese Academy of Sciences — and led me to the question I now care about most: can machines genuinely understand music?

Chasing that question, I led the benchmark team behind ChatMusician (ACL 2024) at M.A.P — curating 1,000+ music-theory QA pairs later folded into the MMLU multimodal subset — and co-authored an ISMIR 2024 study probing whether LLMs can actually reason in music.

Today at BMAT I scale musical version identification: our pipelines doubled the largest dataset in the field while cutting operating costs 10× and storage 30×.
